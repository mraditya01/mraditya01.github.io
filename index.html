Sound Localization and Classification for Self-driving Vehicle

Multiple Moving Agents Systems with physical multiple moving agents will be more and more common, e.g., automated warehouse, delivery service with robots, self-driving cars, etc. Obviously, such systems should be well-coordinated, but in general, coordination/cooperation themselves are ambiguous and intractable, such as societies of ants and bees. Therefore, I believe that multiple moving agents have a lot of applications that we cannot imagine right now. My research questions are twofold: 1) explore the possibility of sound localization on self-driving vehicle, 2) physics data simulation for audio in urban environment.

Keywords : Self-driving vehicle, Sound Localization and Classification, Physics Simulation



Teleoperation System for Robotic Arm

Developed an intuitive teleoperation interface for controlling robotic arms using inverse kinematics. This system enables precise and natural control of robotic manipulators, bridging the gap between human intent and robotic motion. The research focuses on creating an efficient control framework that translates user inputs into real-time robotic arm movements while maintaining accuracy and responsiveness.

Keywords : Robotic Arm, Inverse Kinematics, Teleoperation, Human-Robot Interaction
